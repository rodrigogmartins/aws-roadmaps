## üìö About the Exam

Below is a summary of the main information about the exam. These details, along with more comprehensive information, can be found directly in the [**AIF-C01 Exam Guide**](https://d1.awsstatic.com/onedam/marketing-channels/website/aws/en_US/certification/approved/pdfs/docs-ai-practitioner/AWS-Certified-AI-Practitioner_Exam-Guide.pdf).

### What does the exam validate?

The exam validates the candidate‚Äôs ability to perform the following tasks:

- Understand the concepts, methods, and strategies of AI, ML, and Generative AI in general and within AWS.

- Understand the appropriate use of AI/ML and Generative AI technologies to ask relevant questions within an organization.

- Identify the right types of AI/ML technologies to apply to specific use cases.

- Use AI, ML, and Generative AI technologies responsibly.

### Recommended prior knowledge

Although AWS certifications do not require a specific order, exams may *assume* a certain level of familiarity with related concepts.

Below is a list provided in AWS‚Äôs official documentation for the AI Practitioner certification.

If you feel uncertain about any of these topics, it‚Äôs important to review them before taking the exam ‚Äî even if they initially seem ‚Äúout of scope.‚Äù

- Familiarity with key AWS services (e.g., Amazon EC2, Amazon S3, AWS Lambda, and Amazon SageMaker) and their primary use cases.

- Understanding of the AWS Shared Responsibility Model regarding security and compliance in the AWS Cloud.

- Familiarity with AWS Identity and Access Management (IAM) to secure and control access to AWS resources.

- Understanding of AWS‚Äôs global infrastructure, including Regions, Availability Zones, and Edge Locations.

- Familiarity with AWS service pricing models.

### Content

The exam includes the following content domains and weightings:

- Domain 1: **AI and ML Fundamentals** (20% of scored content)

- Domain 2: **Generative AI Fundamentals** (24% of scored content)

- Domain 3: **Foundation Model Applications** (28% of scored content)

- Domain 4: **Responsible AI Guidelines** (14% of scored content)

- Domain 5: **Security, Compliance, and Governance for AI Solutions** (14% of scored content)
